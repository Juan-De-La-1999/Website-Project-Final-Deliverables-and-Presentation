Rising Stars Muay Thai (RSMT) – MSDM Final Project

About Rising Stars Muay Thai (RSMT)

Rising Stars Muay Thai (RSMT) is a Sacramento-based Muay Thai promotion known for its loyal fighter and fan community, strong local partnerships, and a “fighters-first” matchmaking philosophy. The organization hosts live events and pay-per-view (PPV) fight nights, primarily promoted through social media and Eventbrite.

While RSMT has built a strong grassroots presence and authentic brand, it previously lacked a centralized digital ecosystem, limiting its ability to scale and compete at a higher level.

The Challenge

Prior to this project, RSMT faced several key limitations:

  • No official website or centralized digital platform
  • No structured data collection or analytics infrastructure
  • Limited understanding of audience behavior and customer journey
  • Heavy reliance on Instagram without measurable conversion tracking
  • Disconnected platforms (social media, ticketing, and gym memberships)

These challenges made it difficult to optimize marketing performance, measure ROI, and convert fans into long-term customers.

Digital Transformation & Implementation

Tip

A centralized website was successfully developed and deployed, establishing the foundation for a data-driven marketing strategy.

As part of this project, the following solutions were implemented:

  • A fully functional website hosted on GitHub Pages
  • Google Analytics 4 integration for real-time tracking
  • A Website Traffic Analytics page to monitor and forecast performance
  • A structured framework for collecting and analyzing user behavior

This transformation connects digital touchpoints and enables measurable, data-driven decision-making.

Strategy & Objectives

This project focuses on transforming RSMT into a data-driven organization through six core analytics objectives:

  • Website Optimization: Improve user experience and ticket conversion
  • Audience Segmentation: Identify high-value customer groups
  • Social Media Engagement: Determine content that drives conversions
  • Customer Journey Mapping: Track user behavior from awareness to purchase
  • Event-to-Membership Conversion: Analyze impact of events on gym sign-ups
  • Influencer Impact: Measure effectiveness of fighter-led promotions

The goal is to create a scalable marketing strategy that drives both event attendance and long-term customer growth.

Data Collection & Analytics Framework

A comprehensive data collection strategy has been designed and partially implemented, including:

  • Google Analytics 4: Tracking users, sessions, page views, and engagement
  • Event Data (Eventbrite): Ticket sales and attendance behavior
  • Social Media Insights: Engagement metrics from Instagram and Facebook
  • Customer Data (CRM & Surveys): Demographic and behavioral insights
  • Tracking Systems: Connecting data across platforms using standardized tracking

Additionally, a six-month traffic forecast model was developed to estimate expected website growth as the platform gains visibility.

Impact & Value

This project establishes a foundation for long-term growth by enabling:

  • Data-driven marketing decisions
  • Improved customer targeting and segmentation
  • Increased ticket sales and event attendance
  • Higher conversion to gym memberships
  • Measurable marketing performance and ROI
  • Stronger positioning for sponsorship opportunities

Overall, the project transitions RSMT from intuition-based decision-making to a scalable, analytics-driven growth strategy.

AP Audemars Piguet Re imagining Royal Oak: A Data Driven Case For Innovation

Project Background

This project explored whether satisfaction with fast‑food mobile ordering apps influences how often customers use them and how frequently they make purchases. As mobile ordering becomes a core part of the fast‑food experience, brands struggle to understand whether their apps truly meet customer expectations for speed, convenience, and accuracy. The study focused on identifying which app features drive satisfaction and whether that satisfaction translates into higher usage and repeat purchases—key insights for improving digital experience and customer retention.

Research Approach & Data Analysis

The team designed a Qualtrics survey aligned with two analytics objectives: measuring app satisfaction and linking satisfaction to behavioral outcomes. The survey captured usability, ordering speed, order accuracy, convenience, purchase frequency, and future usage intentions. A sample of 100+ respondents (primarily young adults and students) provided data for SPSS analysis. After cleaning and recoding variables, the team conducted descriptive statistics, correlation testing, and multiple regression. Results showed high satisfaction overall, moderate app usage, and a small positive—but not statistically significant—relationship between satisfaction and usage. Regression analysis indicated that usability factors alone did not significantly predict changes in purchase frequency.

Key Insights & Managerial Impact

The findings suggest that while customers generally enjoy using fast‑food apps, satisfaction alone does not strongly drive purchase frequency. Instead, usage patterns vary widely, especially among younger, mobile‑first consumers. Based on these insights, the team recommended strengthening core app performance (speed, reliability, navigation), implementing loyalty rewards and personalized deals to encourage repeat usage, and targeting marketing efforts toward young adults and students who are most likely to engage with mobile ordering. These strategies help brands reduce friction, increase engagement, and build long‑term digital loyalty.

Does App Satisfaction Drive Fast-Food Purchases

Important

This project analyzes the relationship between mobile app satisfaction and fast-food purchasing behavior using survey data and statistical modeling.

Important

View Presentation

Overview

This project examines whether customer satisfaction with fast-food mobile apps influences purchasing behavior and app usage.

Research Objective

The goal was to understand if higher satisfaction with mobile ordering apps leads to increased purchase frequency and repeat usage.

Methodology

Survey data was collected to measure customer satisfaction and behavior. Key variables included:

  • Ease of use
  • Ordering speed
  • Order accuracy
  • Overall convenience

Statistical methods such as correlation and regression analysis were used to evaluate relationships between satisfaction and purchasing behavior.

Key Findings

  • Customer satisfaction levels were generally high
  • App usage varied across users
  • A positive relationship between satisfaction and usage was observed
  • However, the relationship was not statistically significant

Insights

The results suggest that app satisfaction alone does not strongly drive purchase behavior. Other factors such as promotions, pricing, and brand loyalty likely have a greater influence on purchasing decisions.

Business Implications

  • Improving app usability is important but not sufficient
  • Marketing strategies should focus on incentives and engagement
  • Targeting key demographics (young adults and students) can increase effectiveness
